Lyssa Kay Adams

1 book on Read & Recommend

Writing Style

The Bromance Book Club series keeps coming up in Reddit recommendations for one specific reason: it's funny and self-aware without being smug about it. A baseball player's marriage is falling apart, his teammates run a secret romance novel book club, and they make him read the genre to actually fix his relationship. That premise could easily become a bit — instead, readers describe it as "surprisingly sincere." The book-within-a-book structure is noted as clever without being precious, which is a hard line to walk.

The emotional core is also more complex than a typical romance setup. Readers specifically call out that the central relationship is two people trying to repair something worth saving, not just two people falling for each other. That's a different register, and it shows up in the Reddit threads as the reason people push this on reluctant romance readers.

Where to Start

Start with The Bromance Book Club — it's the one that established the premise and the one readers reach for when recommending the series to romance skeptics. The setup is accessible enough that it works as an entry point even if you've never read contemporary romance. If the Russian hockey player Vlad catches your attention in that book, Isn't It Bromantic? gives him his own story and comes up specifically when readers are looking for a "strange big MMC who gives weirdly cute compliments" — which is a niche that apparently has demand.

Similar Authors

Adams comes up alongside Abby Jimenez, Beth O'Leary, and India Holton in the same recommendation threads — all contemporary romance writers with a comedic or high-concept angle. Graeme Simsion (The Rosie Project) also appears in the same lists, which tracks given the "romance from an unusual male perspective" framing that the Bromance Book Club series leans into.
