Rebecca

by Daphne du Maurier

Rebecca adalah istri pertama Maxim de Winter, kecantikan dan kematiannya yang misterius meninggalkan kenangan muram yang menghantui Manderley, rumah megah tempat dia dulu tinggal. Ketika Maxim menikah lagi dengan seorang wanita muda yang lugu, bayang-bayang Rebecca menghantui Nyonya de Winter yang baru. Kepala rumah tangga Manderley, Nyonya Danvers, menjaga kenangan akan Rebecca dengan pengabdian yang obsesif dan terus mengingatkan Nyonya de Winter yang baru bahwa dia tidak akan pernah sebanding dengan Rebecca. Pengaruh Rebecca dalam kehidupan sehari-hari mereka membuat Nyonya de Winter mulai meragukan kewarasannya dan bahkan suaminya. Penuh ketegangan, kecemburuan, dan psikologi yang mencekam, Rebecca adalah kisah tentang seorang wanita yang terjebak oleh masa lalu, di mana cinta, penipuan, dan kekuatan mengerikan dari ingatan berpadu dalam sebuah klimaks yang dramatis dan menghancurkan.

What Readers Say

Summary of Reader Sentiment: Mentioned alongside Bunny and Secret History as a frequently recommended book in the subreddit. Its inclusion suggests it is a go-to recommendation for atmospheric tension and potentially isolated settings, fitting the general vibe of the community even if not specifically tied to the countryside prompt in this thread.

Cited as the "best and OG thrillers," this book represents the classic, foundational style of the genre. The recommendation implies that if the OP wants to understand what a "good thriller" is at its root, this is the essential starting point, contrasting sharply with the modern, shallow thrillers they currently dislike.

This classic is frequently mentioned as a book readers would never have picked up if not for an assignment or recommendation, specifically because the cover looks like a "sleazy romance novel" with red backgrounds and gold script. Readers emphasize that the book is actually a haunting mystery with a "haunting quality," not a trashy romance. The cover is described as "horrible" and something the designer likely didn't read, leading to missed opportunities for many readers who only read romance.

Commenters focused on the opening line, "Last night I dreamt I went to Maderlay," as the most memorable first line ever, setting a high standard for the book's writing. The description of rhododendrons as "slaughterous" was cited as a moment that hooked readers immediately. The book is praised for its ability to create a strong hook and atmosphere through precise and evocative language. It is recommended for those who appreciate a gripping, well-crafted opening and atmospheric writing.

Mentioned as an all-time favorite alongside We Have Always Lived in the Castle.

Called the poster's "favorite book ever" and "underrated." Readers noted that while it takes a little while to get into (around chapter 13), once hooked, it is impossible to stop reading. It is described as a classic gothic mystery/romance that casts a large shadow over the protagonist's life.
