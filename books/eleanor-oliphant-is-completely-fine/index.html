What Readers Say

Several users specifically named this book as a recommendation. It appears to be a strong contender for readers looking for a story about social awkwardness, isolation, and the journey toward connection, which aligns with the feeling of being behind one's peers.

This recommendation came with a specific trigger warning for child abuse, mental health issues, and a non-graphic suicide attempt. Readers loved the book for being "funny and honestly really awesome" while tackling serious mental health topics. It was described as a strong fit for the theme of living alone.

This novel was recommended for its ability to resonate with themes of healing and finding joy. One commenter explicitly "seconded" the recommendation, indicating strong agreement with its suitability for the thread's request for books about discovering meaning and healing.

This book was suggested as a "great book" and "smart, warm, uplifting" read. However, a significant caveat was raised by a commenter who felt the ending was "going in romantic direction," noting "Idk maybe only me." The Goodreads bot summary included in the thread describes the plot as a journey where the protagonist learns to "open your heart" and find friendship and potentially love. Despite the mixed feedback regarding the romantic ending, multiple users still came to recommend it enthusiastically, with one user buying it specifically to learn about overcoming social anxiety and making peace with their "weirdness."

While noted as "not in the romance genre," this book was recommended for featuring a grumpy character (Eleanor Oliphant) who softens when she befriends a cat (Raymond). Readers described Eleanor as "amazing" and noted that a book club loved it.

While some initial commenters expressed hesitation due to a perceived early focus on a romantic interest, the overall sentiment from other commenters was that the book is "amazing" and "highly recommended." One user noted they regularly think about the book a year after reading it, suggesting a lasting positive impact. The hesitation was framed as a potential misunderstanding of the book's true focus, which is ultimately praised.

Readers found this book impossible to put down, with one user finishing it in a single day and another calling it the "first book in ages that I just couldn't put down." It is described as a "wonderful book" and a great suggestion. One commenter noted the protagonist is in a "very unique mental state" and questioned if it serves as a good general overview of the mundaneness of 20/30s life, but the overall sentiment was highly positive, with users hoping the upcoming mini-series kept the action in Scotland.

Listed in the OP's initial read list.

This book is highly recommended for its portrayal of Eleanor, a main character with a "badly scarred face" dealing with past trauma. Readers loved the book's ability to keep the tone "light and humorous" while exploring heavy themes like loneliness, prejudice, and friendship. Multiple commenters expressed finding parts of themselves in Eleanor, noting that the themes are universal. One reader specifically mentioned discovering the facial scars element while checking the book out, calling it a "gem" and a "fantastic read" that is one of their favorites.

Readers praised this novel as a "lovely" and "incredible" book that sneakily functions as a love story. It is frequently described as a rare read that makes readers both laugh and cry in equal measure. The book fits the prompt perfectly by featuring a protagonist who is socially awkward and plain but eventually forms healthy connections. One reader mentioned reading it in just two days despite having small kids, highlighting its addictive quality. However, a dissenting opinion noted that the titular character is actually the daughter of a model and gets a makeover in the narrative, which contradicts the "no makeover" rule of the prompt, though most readers seem to overlook this or focus on her internal journey.

This book was described as being about an "awkward woman who learns to deal with her demons." It was included in a list of highly recommended books by a user who had read and loved many of the titles in their list, suggesting it is a safe and enjoyable bet for a new reader.

Recommended alongside Anxious People for realistic fiction that deals with suicide and mental health. It is valued for its authentic portrayal of these issues, offering a grounded perspective on pain and healing.

Commenters described this as a wonderful book featuring a very kind male protagonist, though they noted it is "not a traditional romance at all." The relationship develops slowly and naturally. Readers praised the book for its depth, describing it as an "uplifting story without being sappy." It was highlighted as an early entry in the popular category of books featuring socially dysfunctional narrators (specifically mentioning functioning Asperger's). One reader noted it took time to get used to the narrator's quirks but found the book to be an "amazing book on so many levels" that opened possibilities for stories about people who aren't initially attractive but are wonderful underneath.
