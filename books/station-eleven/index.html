Station Eleven

by Emily St. John Mandel

NATIONAL BESTSELLER • NATIONAL BOOK AWARD FINALIST • A PEN/FAULKNER AWARD FINALIST • Set in the eerie days of civilization’s collapse—the spellbinding story of a Hollywood star, his would-be savior, and a nomadic group of actors roaming the scattered outposts of the Great Lakes region, risking everything for art and humanity. • Now an original series on HBO Max. • Over one million copies sold! Kirsten Raymonde will never forget the night Arthur Leander, the famous Hollywood actor, had a heart attack on stage during a production of King Lear. That was the night when a devastating flu pandemic arrived in the city, and within weeks, civilization as we know it came to an end. Twenty years later, Kirsten moves between the settlements of the altered world with a small troupe of actors and musicians. They call themselves The Traveling Symphony, and they have dedicated themselves to keeping the remnants of art and humanity alive. But when they arrive in St. Deborah by the Water, they encounter a violent prophet who will threaten the tiny band’s existence. And as the story takes off, moving back and forth in time, and vividly depicting life before and after the pandemic, the strange twist of fate that connects them all will be revealed. Look for Emily St. John Mandel’s bestselling new novel, Sea of Tranquility!

What Readers Say

Recommended specifically for its post-apocalyptic setting with "vaguely magical realism vibes." Readers praised the author's writing style, with one user stating, "I love the way she writes," and another calling the book "incredible." It was identified as a first thought for multiple users looking for this specific genre blend.

Recommended specifically for its portrayal of the "indomitable human spirit."

Described as "fantastic" and capable of giving readers "the same level of feels as 11/22/63," this book is a dystopian novel set during and after a civilization collapse. The plot involves a Hollywood star's death, a nomadic group of actors (the Traveling Symphony) roaming the Great Lakes region, and themes of art and humanity in a wasteland. The narrative moves back and forth in time, connecting five people through "strange twists of fate." Readers found it spellbinding and elegiac, with one commenter exclaiming it was "so fucking good." It offers a different tone from King's work, focusing on the preservation of culture amidst disaster.

This book is frequently mentioned in the context of "uncanny timing," where the themes of the book mirrored real-world events happening while the reader was consuming it. One reader noted they read it right as the world was changing in early March, while another connected it to the 2016 election. The story of a post-pandemic world is described as unsettling yet compelling, with one reader admitting they were unsure if they could handle the pandemic theme but glad they did. However, opinions are mixed; while many found it a "life-splitting" read, one commenter felt let down by an anticlimactic ending after a big buildup. It is recommended for readers interested in pandemic fiction, time travel, and stories that reflect contemporary anxieties.

This novel received significant praise for its exploration of how small life choices ripple out to affect others, even in the context of a post-apocalyptic setting. Readers noted that despite the potentially anxiety-inducing subject matter (a pandemic), the book was affirming and "absolutely wonderful." Several commenters mentioned re-reading it or finishing it recently, finding the exploration of art and human connection deeply moving. It was described as a "10/10 novel" that deserves more recognition, with users appreciating its depth and emotional resonance without centering the plot on romantic relationships.

This book was praised for placing the reader in a specific "place of mind." One reader described it as "so dreamy, like peering at life through a looking glass." It was noted for making the reader "reassess the things I hold dear/find awe in, and my relationships with those close to me." The emotional impact was profound, with one reader stating they felt compelled to tell everyone they love them "just one more time" immediately after finishing. It captures the beauty of life even amidst a post-apocalyptic setting.

Recommended for its beautiful, melancholic portrayal of life after a pandemic, this book was praised for its focus on art, memory, and human connection in the face of extinction. Readers found it emotionally resonant and well-written.
